## How to choose a robot vacuum for a small flat

Four things decide whether a robot vacuum actually works in a small or rented home. Get these right and the brand matters far less.

1. **Height — can it get under your furniture?** This is the spec most guides skip. A robot only cleans where it fits: under the sofa, the bed, the kickboards. Sub-8 cm models (like the Eufy 11S MAX at 7.2 cm or the Lefant M210 Pro at 7.6 cm) slide under low furniture that taller LiDAR robots (around 9.5 cm, because of the spinning sensor turret on top) bump into and reverse away from.
2. **Noise — your walls are thin.** In a flat or shared house you'll run it while you're home, so loudness matters. Around 51–55 dB is conversational and easy to live with; 66 dB (the Tapo RV30 Max Plus) is closer to a normal vacuum and will carry through a thin wall — fine if you schedule it for when you're out, less so for a shared house.
3. **Dock size — where will it actually live?** A plain charging dock is small and tucks against a skirting board. A self-empty or all-in-one dock is a small bin or cabinet that needs a permanent spot near a socket — a real cost of floor space in a studio. If you go self-empty, the Eufy C10 has the smallest dock here.
4. **Navigation — gyro/random vs LiDAR.** Cheaper robots (Eufy 11S MAX, Roomba 694) move in a semi-random or gyro pattern with no map: simple, low-profile and fine for one or two small rooms, but slower and less systematic. LiDAR robots (Roborock Q5, Tapo RV30) build a map, clean in tidy rows and let you send them to a specific room from the app — more thorough, but taller and dearer. In a one-room flat, random is often enough; over a few rooms, mapping earns its keep.

## Renting? Read this first

A robot vacuum is renter-friendly — no drilling, no fixed install — but a few small choices protect your deposit and your floors:

- **Put the dock near a socket without marking the floor.** The dock needs to sit against a wall by a plug. On laminate or wooden floors, a charging dock that lives in one spot for months can leave marks or scuffs — slip a thin felt pad or an offcut of rug under it, and run the cable along the skirting rather than taping it to a painted wall (tape can pull paint off when you leave).
- **Don't drill anything.** You don't need to. Robot docks and "no-go" zones are set in software (a virtual wall / app map) or with the magnetic boundary strip some models include — never screws or wall fixings in a rental.
- **Be careful with all-in-one / "omni" docks on wooden floors.** The big self-wash docks (and to a lesser extent any mopping robot) hold water and rinse the mop pad. On the wooden or laminate floors common in Irish rentals, a station that sits in one place and handles water can trap moisture underneath over time — stand it on a waterproof mat, empty the dirty-water tank promptly, and don't tuck it into a sealed cupboard. If your tenancy makes you nervous about water near the floor, a vacuum-only robot with a plain charging dock is the safer choice.

## Common mistakes

- **Buying tall, then finding it won't go under the sofa.** Measure the lowest clearance under your furniture *before* choosing — it's the spec that decides where the robot can actually clean.
- **Ignoring noise in a shared home.** A 66 dB robot is great value but loud; schedule it for when you're out, or pick a ~51–52 dB model if you'll run it while home.
- **Forgetting the dock needs a permanent home.** A self-empty station is a small appliance that lives by a socket forever — budget the floor space, or stick with a plain dock.
- **Standing a water-handling dock straight on a rented wooden floor.** Use a waterproof mat and keep it ventilated.

## FAQ

**What's the most important spec for a small flat?**
Height. A robot only cleans where it fits, so a sub-8 cm model that gets under your furniture often beats a taller, "smarter" one that can't.

**Gyro/random or LiDAR navigation — which do I need?**
For a single room or a studio, a gyro/random robot is usually enough and sits lower. Over several rooms, LiDAR mapping cleans more thoroughly and lets you target a specific room — at the cost of more height and money.

**Which of these is quietest?**
The Eufy C10 at ~51 dB, with the Eufy 11S MAX (~51–55 dB) and Lefant M210 Pro (~52 dB) close behind. The Tapo RV30 Max Plus is the loudest at 66 dB.

**Do I need a self-empty dock?**
It's convenient — you empty it every few weeks instead of after most cleans — but the dock takes up floor space. In a small flat, weigh that against a plain charging dock. The Eufy C10 has the smallest self-empty dock here.

**Is a robot vacuum safe for a rented wooden floor?**
The robot itself is fine. The thing to watch is a mopping robot or a water-handling "omni" dock sitting in one spot — stand it on a waterproof mat and keep it ventilated. A vacuum-only robot with a plain dock is the lowest-risk option.

**Will these damage my walls or need drilling?**
No. Boundaries are set in the app or with a magnetic strip — never wall fixings. Run dock cables along the skirting rather than taping them to painted walls.
