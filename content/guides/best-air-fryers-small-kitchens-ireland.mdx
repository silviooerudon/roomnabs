## Quick picks

- **Best overall for tiny kitchens:** Ninja 3.8L Air Fryer (AF100UK)
- **Best if you cook for two:** Ninja Max Pro (AF180UK)
- **Most flexible / packs away:** Ninja Crispi Portable (FN101)
- **Best budget buy:** Morphy Richards 480005

## Ninja 3.8L Air Fryer (AF100UK)

The 3.8-litre AF100UK is the one we point most renters to first. The footprint
is small enough to live on a narrow counter and the single basket is quick to
wash. It comfortably handles chips, a tray of veg or two portions of protein
without dominating the worktop.

## Ninja Max Pro (AF180UK)

A slightly larger basket than the AF100UK, so it's the pick if you regularly
cook for two and want a little more headroom for a small chicken or a fuller
tray. Still a one-plug, sits-on-the-counter appliance.

<CheckPrice linkId="af180uk-currys" retailer="Currys" />

## Ninja Crispi Portable (FN101)

The Crispi is a 4-in-1 portable glass system: the cooking pods double as
storage and serving dishes, so it packs down when you're not using it. Genuinely
useful if your kitchen has zero permanent space to spare.

<CheckPrice linkId="crispi-fn101-euronics" retailer="Euronics" />

## Morphy Richards 480005

The budget pick. It does the air-fryer basics well at a lower price, which makes
it an easy first appliance for a short-term rental you don't want to overspend
on.

<CheckPrice linkId="morphy-480005-currys" retailer="Currys" />

## Philips 2000 Series (NA230)

Philips' 2000 Series is the quiet, no-drama everyday option. If the kitchen
opens onto your living space — as most small flats do — a quieter fan is worth
having.

<CheckPrice linkId="philips-na230-amazon" retailer="Currys" />

## Tefal Easy Fry (EY245840)

The largest basket here, with a dehydrate function on top of air frying. Pick it
if you batch-cook and have just enough room to justify the bigger body.

## How we chose

We prioritised the things that matter in a small rented kitchen: the physical
footprint on the counter, whether it runs from a single socket, how easy the
basket is to hand-wash, and noise. We don't list capacities or prices we
can't verify — always confirm the current spec and price at the retailer before
buying.
